What is LIPI & Why does it exist?
What is LIPI?

LIPI (लिपि) is India's first full-featured programming language written entirely in Devanagari script with Hindi & Sanskrit keywords. Every keyword, every builtin, every error message speaks in the language 600 million Indians read and write every day.

From विधि (function) to यदि/अन्यथा (if/else) to जब तक (while) — the syntax reads like natural Hindi. Variables use Devanagari names, numbers can be written in Hindi digits (१, २, ३…) and even in Indian units (५ लाख = 500,000).

"If a child in Bhopal can learn algebra in Hindi, why should she need to switch to English to write her first program?"
— The core philosophy of LIPI
Why was LIPI built?
  • 🎓 Language Barrier in CS Education 700M Hindi speakers are locked out of programming because every language is in English.
  • 🌍 Cultural Identity in Technology India contributed zero, Fibonacci via Virahanka, trigonometry via Āryabhaṭa — yet our tech speaks English.
  • 🔬 Proof of Concept Demonstrate that a production-quality, bytecode-compiled language can be built in pure Rust with non-Latin syntax.
  • 🤝 Bridge for New Programmers Teach programming concepts to Hindi-medium students without the cognitive load of learning English simultaneously.
